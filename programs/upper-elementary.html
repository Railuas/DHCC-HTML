Upper Elementary Program

Our Upper Elementary community blends a warm, joyful atmosphere with carefully sequenced Montessori lessons. Below you’ll find an in‑depth look at daily life, materials, outcomes, and how to get started. It’s a lot — we want you to have everything you need.

Overview

  • Mixed‑age (9–12) with deeper inquiry, leadership, and real‑world projects.
  • Long‑form reading, writing, and math extensions tailored to readiness.
  • Entrepreneurial, service, and field studies to apply learning authentically.

Ages & Readiness

Placement is individualized. We consider social‑emotional readiness, ability to follow simple routines, and interest in hands‑on exploration. We partner closely with families during the transition period.

Curriculum & Materials

  • Math: long division, fractions/decimals, geometry proofs & measurement.
  • Language: literature circles, research papers, speeches & debates.
  • Science: design‑build engineering, labs, data collection & analysis.
  • Community: micro‑economy, service projects, student leadership.

Daily Rhythm

  1. Goal‑setting & advisory
  2. Seminars, lessons, and independent work
  3. Labs, projects, and field work
  4. Clubs/electives and service
  5. Showcase & weekly reflection

Outcomes

  • Academic resilience and critical thinking
  • Leadership, collaboration, and mentoring younger students
  • Purpose, initiative, and real‑world competence

Parent Partnership

We host regular observations, workshops, and conferences. You’ll receive guidance on supporting independence at home — from practical life to reading together — so school and home feel aligned.

FAQs

If your child is curious, enjoys choosing activities, and benefits from calm, consistent routines, Montessori is often a great fit. We also welcome visits so you can observe the environment.

Older students model leadership and teach concepts they’ve mastered; younger students learn by observation and receive targeted lessons at their level.

We use ongoing observation, work journals, and periodic benchmarks. Progress conferences share growth across academic, social, and practical life domains.

In Elementary, students may have light follow‑ups or reading. We emphasize mastery through hands‑on work and projects rather than frequent tests.

Enrollment Steps

  1. Submit an inquiry form and schedule a tour/observation.
  2. Complete the application and family questionnaire.
  3. Student visit (classroom or playdate, depending on age).
  4. Enrollment decision and placement discussion.
  5. Finalize paperwork, uniforms (if any), and start date.

What to Bring

  • Reusable water bottle with name
  • Healthy snack (if not provided) and labeled lunch (Elementary)
  • Indoor shoes (closed‑toe)
  • Change of clothes in a labeled pouch (Toddler/Pre‑School)
  • Weather‑appropriate outerwear and sun protection
